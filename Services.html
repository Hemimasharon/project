Streaming services

Major streaming services offer extensive anime libraries, with varying specializations.

Crunchyroll:

One of the most complete anime streaming services, with a large library that includes titles from the Funimation catalog. It is known for its simulcasts, where episodes are released shortly after they air in Japan.

Netflix:

Known for its high-quality original anime productions and its extensive international reach. The platform offers a wide range of popular anime series and films, often with multiple language and subtitle options.

Hulu:

Offers a strong selection of classic and popular titles, in addition to its broader live and on-demand streaming content.

Hidive:

A service that offers exclusive anime titles, including uncensored and mature content.

Amazon Prime Video:

Features a substantial anime catalog, with some exclusive titles and partnerships with other services, such as Crunchyroll through Prime Video Channels.

Tubi and RetroCrush:

Free, ad-supported streaming services that are excellent for budget-conscious fans. Tubi has a general library, while RetroCrush focuses on older, classic anime.
